The problem

Today, engineers burn hours iterating CAD concepts by hand, tweaking sketches, and re‑running simulations just to reach a first viable design. Early‑stage exploration is slow, expensive, and rarely covers more than a handful of options.

Manual iteration

Designers redraw similar parts again and again instead of exploring higher‑level ideas and trade‑offs.

Narrow design space

Time pressure means only a few variants are tested, so better concepts are left undiscovered.

Tool friction

Complex CAD workflows make it hard for non‑experts to participate in early design discussions.

The GenCAD solution

GenCAD uses generative AI to produce CAD‑ready design concepts from natural‑language prompts plus engineering constraints. Instead of drawing everything from scratch, you start from a family of optimized options.

Prompt‑driven concepts

Describe the function, constraints, and manufacturing method in plain English; GenCAD proposes candidate geometries.

Text‑to‑CAD Design intent

Constraint‑aware AI

Specify loads, materials, safety factors, and process limits; the system optimizes for weight, stiffness, or cost.

Lightweighting Safety factors

CAD‑friendly outputs

Export candidate designs as editable CAD (e.g. STEP / feature‑based templates) so engineers can refine them in existing tools.

How it fits your workflow

GenCAD is designed to sit before detailed CAD modeling. It accelerates early exploration while keeping engineers in control of final geometry and sign‑off.

Step 1

Describe the part

Capture function, interfaces, loads, constraints, and manufacturing preferences in a structured prompt.

Step 2

Set constraints

Define materials, safety factors, maximum mass, and forbidden regions or keep‑out zones.

Step 3

Generate variants

Let the AI explore many geometries, then filter by performance metrics and visual similarity.

Step 4

Export & refine

Send shortlisted concepts into your preferred CAD package for detailed modelling and validation.
